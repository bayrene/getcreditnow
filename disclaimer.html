Affiliate Disclosure

GetCreditNow.com is a participant in affiliate programs, including the National Debt Relief affiliate program through ShareASale. This means that when you click on certain links on our site and are accepted into a debt relief program, we may earn a commission.

This comes at absolutely no additional cost to you. The services you receive and the prices you pay are the same whether you arrived through our site or directly. Our affiliate relationships do not increase the cost of any product or service for our readers.

We recommend services based on thorough research, the provider's reputation, accreditations, and our genuine desire to help our readers find financial relief. However, our affiliate relationships may influence which services we choose to review or recommend. We strive to be transparent about this at all times.

We do not guarantee any specific outcomes from using the services or strategies discussed on this site. Debt relief results vary based on individual circumstances including:

  • Market conditions and creditor policies

When we cite potential savings (such as "up to 50% debt reduction"), these represent typical program results as reported by providers but are not guaranteed for any individual. Your actual results may be higher or lower.

Credit Score Impact

Some of the debt relief strategies and programs discussed on this site may negatively affect your credit score. Debt settlement, in particular, typically has a negative short-term impact on credit scores. We strive to clearly disclose these potential impacts in our content, but you should thoroughly research and understand any credit implications before enrolling in any program.

Tax Implications

Forgiven or settled debt may be considered taxable income by the IRS. If more than $600 of debt is forgiven, your creditor may issue a 1099-C form. Consult a qualified tax professional to understand the potential tax implications of any debt relief strategy.
